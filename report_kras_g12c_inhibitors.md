## Section 2: Competitive Analysis

### 2.1 Overview of Active KRAS G12C Programs

Eleven distinct Phase 3 trials across six drug candidates and five sponsors are currently active or recently completed. The competitive landscape divides into three strategic segments:

#### **Segment A: Established Monotherapy (2L+)**
- **Sotorasib (CodeBreaK 200, NCT04303780)** — Amgen's first-mover program, now ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING, completed primary analysis Aug 2, 2022. Published results (de Langen et al., *Lancet* 2023) demonstrate superiority on PFS over docetaxel. Recent 2025 publication (Dingemans et al.) reports intracranial activity data.
  - **Competitive position:** Established regulatory precedent (FDA approved 2021); earliest readout; no ongoing recruitment.

- **Adagrasib (KRYSTAL-12, NCT04685135)** — Mirati's parallel 2L+ monotherapy program, completed primary analysis Dec 30, 2023. Published results (Barlesi et al., *Lancet* 2025) show efficacy against docetaxel.
  - **Competitive position:** Published comparable efficacy to sotorasib; ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING status suggests primary readout mature.

#### **Segment B: 1L Combination Therapy (Emerging Readouts)**

**Sotorasib-based:**
- **CodeBreaK 202 (NCT05920356)** — Amgen's 1L combination trial (sotorasib + platinum doublet vs. pembrolizumab + platinum doublet) in PD-L1 negative nonsquamous NSCLC. Currently RECRUITING with primary completion Jun 30, 2027. No publications yet.
  - **Differentiator:** Targets PD-L1 negative population (typically excluded from PD-1 monotherapy); platinum-based chemotherapy backbone.
  - **Timing:** ~2.5 year readout timeline from trial start (Nov 2023).

**Adagrasib-based:**
- **KRYSTAL-7 (NCT04613596)** — Mirati's 1L combination (adagrasib + pembrolizumab vs. pembrolizumab alone) in PD-L1 TPS ≥50%. Mixed Phase 2/3 design; currently RECRUITING. Phase 3 primary completion Jun 9, 2032. No publications yet.
  - **Differentiator:** High PD-L1 population; simpler (non-chemotherapy) combination; longer follow-up.

- **KRYSTAL-4 (NCT06875310)** — Mirati's 1L combination (adagrasib + pembrolizumab + chemotherapy vs. placebo + pembrolizumab + chemotherapy) in nonsquamous NSCLC. Currently RECRUITING with primary completion Sep 7, 2029. Double-blind design. No publications yet.
  - **Differentiator:** Triple combination (KRAS inhibitor + checkpoint inhibitor + chemotherapy); most aggressive approach; blinded design enhances rigor.

![Trial NCT06875310](screenshots/NCT06875310.png)

**Divarasib-based:**
- **NCT06497556** — Hoffmann-La Roche's 2L+ comparison (divarasib vs. sotorasib/adagrasib). ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING; primary completion Sep 30, 2027. Head-to-head vs. established agents.
  - **Differentiator:** Direct competitive trial design (vs. both sotorasib AND adagrasib); tests whether Roche's next-generation G12C inhibitor offers advantage; late-stage enrollment.

![Trial NCT06497556](screenshots/NCT06497556.png)

- **NCT06793215** — Hoffmann-La Roche's 1L combination (divarasib + pembrolizumab vs. pembrolizumab + chemotherapy) in nonsquamous NSCLC. RECRUITING with primary completion Nov 30, 2028. PFS and OS co-primary endpoints.
  - **Differentiator:** 1L setting; non-chemotherapy backbone (pembrolizumab + chemotherapy comparator); longest follow-up expected (~5 years).

![Trial NCT05920356](screenshots/NCT05920356.png)

**Olomorasib (Eli Lilly):**
- **SUNRAY-02 (NCT06890598)** — Novel, two-part design: Part A (resected KRAS G12C+ NSCLC, olomorasib + pembrolizumab vs. pembrolizumab + placebo, primary endpoint DFS) and Part B (unresectable, olomorasib + durvalumab vs. durvalumab + placebo, primary endpoint PFS). Currently RECRUITING with primary completion May 2029. Double-blind, placebo-controlled.
  - **Differentiator:** Adjuvant setting (Part A, resected disease); use of durvalumab (not pembrolizumab) in Part B; disease-free survival as coprimary; represents Lilly's first KRAS G12C in NSCLC.

#### **Segment C: Emerging/Regional Programs**

**D-1553 (Chia Tai Tianqing, China-based):**
- **NCT06300177** — 2L+ monotherapy (D-1553 vs. docetaxel). RECRUITING; primary completion Oct 2026. Double-blind, double-simulated design.
  - **Competitive position:** Chinese sponsor; earliest 2L+ readout in this cohort (Oct 2026, vs. Sep 2027–Sep 2029 for others); enrolling Asian population.

- **NCT07174908** — 1L combination with IN10018 (anti-PD-1, partner sponsor InxMed, Shanghai). RECRUITING; primary completion Sep 2028. First-line nonsquamous setting.
  - **Competitive position:** Regional (China-focused); combination with partnered anti-PD-1; distinct biomarker/population strategy vs. Western trials.

**Daraxonrasib (Revolution Medicines):**
- **RASolve 301 (NCT06881784)** — Pan-RAS(ON) inhibitor (targets RAS G12X-C, including G12C but broader scope). 2L+ setting vs. docetaxel. RECRUITING; primary completion Dec 1, 2027.
  - **Competitive position:** Broader mechanistic scope (not KRAS G12C-selective); earliest readout in this group (Dec 2027); pan-RAS strategy may differentiate if G12C-selective agents plateau.

### 2.3 Strategic Differentiation

| Dimension | Key Observations |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Monotherapy vs. Combination** | Established monotherapy market (CodeBreaK 200, KRYSTAL-12) now maturing. Shift to 1L combinations: all newer programs (2024–2025 starts) are combination designs. |
| **Checkpoint Inhibitor Choice** | Pembrolizumab dominates (CodeBreaK 202, KRYSTAL-7, KRYSTAL-4, NCT06793215, SUNRAY-02 Part A). Durvalumab (SUNRAY-02 Part B, adjuvant) is sole alternative. |
| **Chemotherapy Backbone** | CodeBreaK 202 and KRYSTAL-4 include platinum doublet; NCT06793215 (Roche) compares divarasib + pembrolizumab against pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (indirect comparison). D-1553 and daraxonrasib remain monotherapy-focused. |
| **Population Stratification** | CodeBreaK 202 targets **PD-L1 negative** (rare, underserved); KRYSTAL-7 requires **PD-L1 TPS ≥50%** (narrow); others unselected or standard nonsquamous. |
| **Adjuvant Setting** | SUNRAY-02 (Eli Lilly) is the **only adjuvant program** in this cohort (resected disease); potential differentiation if efficacy shown. |
| **Geographic Scope** | D-1553 and IN10018 programs are primarily China-enrolled; Western Big Pharma (Amgen, Mirati, Roche, Lilly) target global or North American populations. |
| **Biomarker Selectivity** | All trials enroll KRAS G12C+; daraxonrasib (RASolve 301) uniquely targets **RAS G12X-C** (broader), creating potential post-hoc subset analysis vs. pure G12C programs. |

### 2.4 Publication Status & Evidence Maturity

- **Mature monotherapy (published):** CodeBreaK 200 (sotorasib), KRYSTAL-12 (adagrasib). Both have Lancet publications (2023, 2025).
- **Immature 1L combinations (no publications):** All active 1L combination trials (CodeBreaK 202, KRYSTAL-4, KRYSTAL-7, NCT06793215, SUNRAY-02, NCT07174908) lack peer-reviewed readouts.
- **Implication:** Competitive information is primarily from completed 2L+ monotherapy programs; 1L combination landscape is entirely speculative until 2027–2029 readouts.

## Section 3: Limitations and Caveats

### 3.1 Trials Excluded & Reasons

1. **NCT03178552** (Hoffmann-La Roche, "Multiple Targeted Therapies in NSCLC") — Returned by initial search but **excluded because** it is listed as Phase 2/3 with start date 2017-09-22 and status ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING. This is a platform/umbrella trial structure; divarasib's involvement is unclear, and it does not appear to be a dedicated KRAS G12C Phase 3 program. No clear primary completion date or KRAS G12C-specific endpoints in summary.

2. **Opnurasib (JDQ443) trials** — Searches for "opnurasib JDQ443 NSCLC Phase 3" returned no results. Hoffmann-La Roche's JDQ443 program may be in Phase 2 or early preclinical; if active Phase 3 trials exist, they did not surface in ClinicalTrials.gov or use non-standard terminology.

3. **Pan-RAS programs (non-G12C selective, early stage)** — Broader RAS inhibitor programs (e.g., SOS1i, pan-RAS(ON)) that may include NSCLC were not specifically searched; focus remained on KRAS G12C-selective agents per task scope.

### 3.2 ClinicalTrials.gov Data Limitations

1. **Efficacy Numbers Not Retrievable:** Primary and secondary endpoints are **listed as measure titles** (e.g., "PFS," "OS") but **do NOT include hazard ratios, response rates, or median survival times**. All such data must come from PubMed publications, which are absent for most 1L combination trials.
   - *Example:* NCT06875310 (KRYSTAL-4) lists "Progression Free Survival (PFS)" as primary endpoint but ClinicalTrials.gov provides no numeric readout or interim safety data.

2. **Incomplete Comparator Details:** Chemotherapy backbones (e.g., "platinum doublet," "pemetrexed + carboplatin/cisplatin") are sometimes vague. Exact dosing, scheduling, and switch criteria are not consistently provided in summary data.

3. **Biomarker Cutoffs:** PD-L1 thresholds (e.g., "TPS ≥50%" in KRYSTAL-7) are stated in titles/summaries but confirmation of actual enrollment cutoffs is not always explicit in trial details.

4. **Publication Lag:** Trials with primary completion dates in 2022–2023 (CodeBreaK 200, KRYSTAL-12) have published; 2025–2029 primary completion dates have no publications yet—expected, but limits current competitive evidence.

### 3.3 Suspected Gaps & Unknowns

1. **Additional Amgen Programs:** Amgen has approved sotorasib (Lumakras, 2021) and may have unreported Phase 3 trials in other KRAS G12C-positive cancers (e.g., colorectal, pancreatic) or combination permutations in NSCLC not yet registered or visible on ClinicalTrials.gov at the time of search.

2. **Hoffmann-La Roche Additional Trials:** Roche's extensive KRAS G12C portfolio (divarasib, other candidates in preclinical phase) may include Phase 3 programs not yet posted or with alternative trial acronyms not captured by searches.

3. **Mirati Takeover by Bristol-Myers Squibb:** Mirati Therapeutics was acquired by Bristol-Myers Squibb in 2024. Legacy Mirati trials (KRYSTAL series) may be re-registered or restructured under BMS sponsorship; current ClinicalTrials.gov entries reflect Mirati as sponsor, but future updates may change this, requiring re-search.

4. **Regional/Emerging Sponsor Programs:** Chinese, Indian, or other regional KRAS G12C inhibitors may be in Phase 3 but not yet registered on ClinicalTrials.gov (US registry); this search captured D-1553 and IN10018 but likely missed others.

5. **Biomarker-Stratified Subtrials:** Some Phase 3 programs may have **post-hoc biomarker analyses** or **pre-specified subgroup trials** (e.g., STK11/KEAP1 loss, TP53 status, PD-L1 levels) that are registered separately; this search did not specifically target such substudies.

6. **Uncertainty on Daraxonrasib (RASolve 301) Scope:** The trial targets "RAS G12X-C" (RAS G12X excluding G12C), which is broader than pure G12C. Primary endpoint is PFS/OS in the RAS G12X-C subgroup; whether G12C-specific secondary endpoints or biomarker-matched arms exist is unclear from summary data.

### 3.4 Search Strategy Completeness

- **Searches conducted:** 6 broad/class searches + 11 drug/trial name searches + 4 sponsor searches = 21 distinct queries.
- **Deduplication:** 11 unique NCT IDs identified across all searches; no additional trials emerged from sponsor-filtered searches (Amgen, Mirati, Roche, Eli Lilly, Revolution Medicines all recovered via drug/trial name searches).
- **Uncertainty:** Trials using non-standard nomenclature, regional/Chinese registries, or pre-registration-phase programs are likely underrepresented.
